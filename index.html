Don't ask to ask!

Every now and then, in online chat rooms, and Discord servers, someone pops up and says something along the lines of:

hey guys how do i download and boot and install ubuntu on my acer aspire 1 with 500gb ram and and a 12 core processor with 24 hours of battery life and how do i make it so firefox functions normally like on windows

This is bad form, for several reasons. What the person is actually asking here is:

Are there any people here willing to answer my question, but I don't feel like even formatting it properly, doing my research, or adding a question mark at the end?

There are plenty of reasons why people who do have the knowledge would not answer the question. By asking the question like this, you're asking for more than you think you're asking for.
You're asking people to take the time to work out what you're actually saying. You're making reason for people not to answer. You're showing that you don't actually appreciate the help people are voluntarily offering. You're also unnecessarily makinig people struggle to even read the question.
Alternatively, the question can be seen as:

I have a question about [blank] but I'm too lazy to actually phrase it in readable words.

This is just lazy. If you're not willing to do the work to solve your problem, why should we?
So format your question, and use proper punctuation. Someone who is idling on the channel and only every now and then glances at what's going on is unlikely to answer your mess of words, but your actual readable question may pique their interest and get them to answer.
So, to summarize, don't ask "please help me with java i need help with log4j its giving me troubles", but rather ask "How do I do [problem] with Java and [other relevant info]? Some ways to reproduce this issue are: [blank], and here are some logs: [blank].".
Sometimes reproducible steps might not be relevant, and you may not know how to get the logs. You should at least acknowledge that you know that log files are relevant, and you should ask how to get those logs as part of your question.
